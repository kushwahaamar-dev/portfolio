I’m a computer-science student at Texas Tech, nerd-sniped by two things I haven’t been able to put down: getting AI agents to prove they actually behaved, and simulating how a seizure spreads through the brain. The first became real software — a secret scanner people install from npm, contracts that slash an agent’s stake when it breaks its word, a payment that only clears for work it can prove. The second runs the model used to plan epilepsy surgery, about a thousand times faster.

Off the clock: globally ranked in Mobile Legends, and 500+ manhwa and light novels deep. Same stubbornness, different ladder.

Now June 2026 — Applied Scientist Intern at Tempora Labs this summer, on agentic AI. The rest of my time goes to the epilepsy lab, where we’re testing whether cognitive effort can quiet a seizure before it spreads.
